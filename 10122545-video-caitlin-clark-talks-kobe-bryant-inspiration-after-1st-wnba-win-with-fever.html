After helping the Indiana Fever secure the team's first win of the season on Friday night, Caitlin Clark discussed how NBA legend Kobe Bryant has inspired her.

"I guess that's just the mentality you have to have as a competitor," Clark said. "It's just, you want those moments, you embrace those moments when your team needs a big shot. I was able to create a little separation and get the shot off. Would have been nice to hit a few more of those tonight, but we got the job done when we needed to."

The 22-year-old was wearing the Lakers legend's shoes during Indiana's victory over the Los Angeles Sparks at Crypto.com Arena, Bryant's old stomping grounds. Clark hasn't been shy about her preference for the shoes, calling them the "best things in the world" after Friday's game.

While she shot just 4-of-14 from the field against Los Angeles, she still finished with an impressive all-around game by recording a career-high 10 rebounds and dishing out eight assists to go along with her 11 points.

With the Fever clinging onto a two-point lead and just over 40 seconds remaining in the fourth quarter, Clark buried a deep step-back triple to give Indiana some breathing room and help secure the victory.
