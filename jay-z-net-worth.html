Throughout the late 1990s and early 2000s, Jay-Z continued to release chart-topping songs and albums, consistently earning millions through his music career.     Jay-Z’s Business Ventures and Investments

    In addition to his music career, Jay-Z has made shrewd business investments that have contributed to his net worth. He launched the clothing line Rocawear in 1999, which became a leading brand in hip-hop fashion and was sold for hundreds of millions in 2007. Jay-Z also bought a percentage of the Brooklyn Nets in 2003 and later sold his stake to start the sports agency Roc Nation Sports. He ventured into the streaming music business in 2015 with the acquisition of Tidal, which he sold in 2021. Jay-Z has also earned millions from his liquor brands, Armand de Brignac and D’Usse.

    One of Jay-Z’s most notable investments was his acquisition of Tidal, a music streaming service competing with industry giants like Spotify and Apple Music. In 2015, Jay-Z purchased Tidal for $56 million and strategically positioned the platform as an artist-owned service, offering higher royalty rates for creators. Under his leadership, Tidal became known for its exclusive releases and high-quality audio streaming, attracting both music lovers and industry professionals.

Jay-Z used this opportunity to expand his influence beyond music and leverage his brand for strategic partnerships in the sports industry.
